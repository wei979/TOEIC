Credit card fraud is becoming less common in our society thanks to major credit card providers being more vigilant and offering better fraud protection. Instead of just requiring signatures, many cards now come equipped with an internal chip and require a password to be used. Credit card providers send regular emails to users regarding any suspicious activity, such as unusual large purchases, or purchases attempts in unusual locations. Often times, the card company may even freeze the account in such cases, until the users calls in to verify. Signing in to online accounts requires safe passwords that are long and include letters, numerals, and punctuation points, and you may have to answer some obscure personal questions as a form of verification.
